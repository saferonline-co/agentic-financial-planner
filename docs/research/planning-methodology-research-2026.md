# Briefing: Modeling a Goal-Based Retirement Plan with a Passive Core + an Active, Illiquid Property Sleeve

A methodology brief for a couple planning toward a target retirement year.

## 1. Bucket / time-segmentation strategies
- Evensky Cash-Flow Reserve (two-bucket): a Cash-Flow Reserve (cash/MM for near-term spending) + an Investment Portfolio (total return). Original 2yrs reserve; Texas Tech (Salter et al.) found ~1yr optimal (more cash = return drag). Refill opportunistically from the IP in up-markets/at rebalancing, never force-sell equities in a downturn. Source: Pfeiffer, Salter & Evensky, FPA Journal 2013 (https://www.financialplanningassociation.org/article/journal/SEP13-benefits-cash-reserve-strategy-retirement-distribution-planning).
- Three-bucket (Benz/Morningstar; Lucia): Bucket1 ~2yr cash; Bucket2 ~5-8yr bonds/TIPS; Bucket3 remainder equities. Benz refill = threshold rebalancing (trim a position >110% of target, route to spending then refill B1); rejects mechanical 3→2→1 cascading. Sources: https://aaiila.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/C-Benz-Bucket-Strategy.pdf , https://www.morningstar.com/portfolios/bucket-approach-building-retirement-portfolio
- DISAGREEMENT (flag): academic weight says bucketing does NOT improve outcomes vs a rebalanced total-return portfolio of the same allocation. Kitces: with annual rebalancing a bucket strategy is mathematically identical to static allocation ("asset-allocation mirage"); without rebalancing it underperforms (https://www.kitces.com/blog/are-retirement-bucket-strategies-an-asset-allocation-mirage/). Estrada (IESE, 115-yr/21-country): bucketing had HIGHER failure rates in the 50-70% equity range, destroys wealth via cash drag (https://blog.iese.edu/jestrada/files/2019/09/BucketApproach.pdf). Tomlinson (middle ground): roughly a wash; behavioral benefit real if cheap (https://www.marketwatch.com/story/is-a-bucket-strategy-superior-to-the-4-rule-11626210623).
- Takeaway: bucketing's value is BEHAVIORAL, not return-enhancing. Adopt Kitces' resolution: invest as total-return, REPORT as buckets. Don't let mental accounting distort allocation.

## 2. Goal-based / liability-driven investing: the floor, and the dated liability
- Floor-and-upside / safety-first (Pfau, Zwecher): cover essential spend with a guaranteed/low-risk income floor; take market risk only with the surplus. A secured floor frees the upside portfolio to be more aggressive. Pfau RISA 2x2 (Probability-based vs Safety-first × Optionality vs Commitment) → Total Return, Income Protection, Risk Wrap, Time Segmentation. Sources: https://www.kitces.com/blog/risa-framework-retirement-income-planning-client-preferences-total-return-strategy-risk-tolerance/ , https://retirementincomejournal.com/article/floor-it/
- CRITICAL distinction — a recurring STREAM vs a single dated LUMP SUM:
  - Income FLOOR (capital-preservation goal): recurring annual essential spend + longevity → cash-flow-matched ladder (TIPS/index-linked, staggered maturities); lifetime annuities (SPIA/DIA) add mortality credits + longevity protection a ladder can't. Residual risk: longevity past the ladder horizon.
  - Single dated LIABILITY (a house paid off at the target year): one known amount on one date → a single held-to-maturity zero-coupon / index-linked bond maturing on the date. Solves it with no reinvestment/price risk. Do NOT use annuity/mortality arguments here.
  - Sources: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dedicated_portfolio_theory , https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/professional-learning/refresher-readings/2026/liability-driven-index-based-strategies , https://retirementresearcher.com/building-retirement-income-tips-ladder/
- Criticisms to keep honest: opportunity cost vs equities; annuity fees/illiquidity; inflation erosion in nominal ladders (favor inflation-linked). Longevity gap in a bond-only floor is real — only annuities truly close it.

## 3. Modeling the active, illiquid, lumpy property sleeve
- Core-satellite sizing by RISK not dollars: 70-90% passive core / 10-30% active satellite (80/20 modal). Size the sleeve so −100% of it is survivable; a levered single development can go to zero equity, so the dollar size IS the loss budget. Back-solve dollar allocation from a tolerable max-loss on net worth; enforce as a HARD cap (CFA warns unconstrained optimization gives unstable PE-dominated corner solutions). Sources: https://www.cfainstitute.org/insights/professional-learning/refresher-readings/2026/asset-allocation-to-alternative-investments , https://analystprep.com/study-notes/frm/var-and-risk-budgeting-in-investment-management/
- Do NOT use smooth annual-return Monte Carlo for the sleeve. Hybrid method: (1) deal-level scenario/decision tree with discrete branches (entitlement-fail / build-and-sell-at-loss / base sale / hot-market), each probability × payoff, EMV = Σ(Pi×Payoffi), each branch from levered IRR + equity-multiple with stressed inputs (5-10% cost-overrun contingency, exit/cap sensitivity, financing-rate sensitivity, delays). Dev benchmarks: opportunistic ground-up IRR 15%+, equity multiple 2.5x+, target 15-20% margin on cost. (2) Inject the outcome distribution into the household Monte Carlo as a DATED LUMP-SUM in the exit year, kept as a separate stream (timing + all-or-nothing tail matter). Sources: https://deckary.com/blog/decision-tree-analysis , https://www.phoenixstrategy.group/blog/irr-roi-metrics-for-real-estate-development
- Survivability gating: CFA — <15yr horizon should generally avoid private RE/PE. Kitces — illiquidity premium is "a privilege not a right," only for those with ample liquid funds elsewhere. Rule: property sleeve funded EXCLUSIVELY from surplus above the safe floor, never floor capital. Sources: https://www.kitces.com/blog/capturing-an-excess-return-premium-for-illiquidity-is-a-privilege-not-a-right/ , https://retirementresearcher.com/what-is-a-safety-first-retirement-plan/

## 4. Sequence-of-returns risk and the pension bridge (target retirement year → first state-pension year)
- Sequence risk peaks in the "fragile decade" around retirement (portfolio largest, withdrawals begin, short recovery runway). Correlation of first ~15yr cumulative real return with sustainable withdrawal rate ≈ 0.79. Source: https://www.kitces.com/blog/managing-portfolio-size-effect-with-bond-tent-in-retirement-red-zone/
- Fund the bridge: bridge = (annual income gap) × (gap years). Ring-fence a duration-matched ladder (gilts/Treasuries/CDs), one rung per year from the retirement year to the first state-pension year, held to maturity. No equities in bridge money.
- Bond tent (Pfau & Kitces): the bridge ladder is the spend-down arm of a rising-equity glidepath — bonds peak at retirement (~30% equities) then rise to ~60-70% over ~15yrs. A 30%→70% glidepath beat static 60/40 (95.1% vs 93.2% success) at lower average equity. Source: https://www.financialplanningassociation.org/article/journal/JAN14-reducing-retirement-risk-rising-equity-glide-path
- Caveat: naive multi-year cash buffers "don't work unless you're a good market timer" (Kitces); a duration-matched ladder sized to the gap is the disciplined version that does work; prefer dynamic spending guardrails for non-bridged spend. Source: https://www.kitces.com/blog/research-reveals-cash-reserve-strategies-dont-work-unless-youre-a-good-market-timer/
- Defer the illiquid/risky sleeve until after the fragile decade where possible. (State-pension note: for a frozen-while-abroad pension the bridge is about funding the gap to the pension start year, not optimizing deferral.)

## 5. Handling a high-probability-but-uncertain inheritance
- Consensus: "hope for it, don't depend on it." Exclude from BASE case; plan must work at zero. Source: https://www.kiplinger.com/retirement/dont-count-on-inheritance-for-your-retirement-plan
- Refinement for a high-probability material windfall: three discrete scenarios — Base (zero), Moderate (~50% haircut, late arrival), Upside (full, sooner). Tie to wants/wishes never needs. Present as the DELTA between scenarios, not a base-case pillar. EV-weighting fine for prioritization but don't collapse the downside out of view. Sources: https://www.talismanwealthadvisors.com/how-to-plan-for-an-inheritance-when-the-amount-is-uncertain , https://www.cpbj.com/how-proposes-plan-financially-uncertain-inheritances/

## 6. Presenting alternatives for a decision
- Don't reduce a strategy to one probability-of-success number (Blanchett/CFA + Kitces): it's binary, ignores magnitude of failure and adjustability. Source: https://rpc.cfainstitute.org/blogs/enterprising-investor/2023/rethinking-outcome-metrics-for-financial-planning
- Report a vector per goal per strategy: (1) probability of success AND probability+magnitude of adjustment (give trigger value + size of spend change) https://www.kitces.com/blog/monte-carlo-guardrails-probability-of-adjustment-success-client-communication-dynamic-retirement-spending/ ; (2) funded ratio (assets ÷ PV liabilities) https://retirementresearcher.com/funded-ratio-vs-monte-carlo-simulations-whats-the-best-way-to-plan-for-retirement/ ; (3) magnitude+timing of worst-case shortfall; (4) liquidity through the bridge; (5) bequest/terminal wealth. Report each goal separately under needs/wants/wishes priority.

## Recommended framework
Floor-and-upside with three engines, inheritance excluded from base. Engine 1 (floor): defease the dated house payoff with a single held-to-maturity zero-coupon/index-linked instrument maturing in the payoff year; build the capital-preservation income floor from the locked passive bucket as it unlocks + index-linked income; treat the pension bridge (retirement year → first state-pension year) as a ring-fenced duration-matched gilt ladder (one rung/yr) doubling as the bond-tent spend-down arm of a rising-equity glidepath. Engine 2 (upside): invest the passive core total-return, report as buckets, sized so essentials never depend on it. Engine 3 (active sleeve): model property dev as a deal-level decision tree (fail/loss/base/hot from levered IRR + stressed inputs), inject as a dated lump-sum into the household Monte Carlo as a separate stream, funded only from liquid surplus above floor + reserve, capped so a wipeout costs ≤ a preset fraction of net worth, deferred until after the fragile decade. Run base case with inheritance = zero; layer moderate (~50% haircut, late) and upside as deltas. Present strategies side-by-side on a vector of metrics per goal.
Caveats: (1) bucketing/cash-buffers are behavioral not return-enhancing — implement as reporting/duration-matching discipline; (2) keep the dated-liability technique (zero/ladder for the house) separate from the income-floor technique (ladder + possible annuity) — conflating them is the most common error.
